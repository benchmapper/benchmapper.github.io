Benchmapper Help

Benchmapper allows users to quickly find benches on OpenStreetMap.
It also helps OpenStreetMap contributors identify benches that haven't yet been added to OpenStreetMap.

Getting started with OpenStreetMap

Create an account at OpenStreetMap.org.

Tip: don't use spaces in your account name. Not all OpenStreetMap editors support spaces.

Using a desktop or laptop

On a desktop or laptop computer, use the web-based editor on OpenStreetMap.org.

iPhone

The OpenStreetMap website does not display on mobile, so you'll need to download and install an app.

The best OpenStreetMap editor for iPhone is "Go Map!!", spelled with two exclamation marks. Download Go Map!! from the Apple iOS App Store.

Android

The OpenStreetMap website does not display on mobile, so you'll need to download and install an app.

The most popular OpenStreetMap editor for Android is Vespucci. Download Vespucci from the Google Play store or on the Vespucci website.

Adding images

The most popular website for hosting photos for OpenStreetMap is Flickr.

Adding the image tag as a preset in Go Map!!

Making changes

Once you've approved changes, you still have to save them to the OpenStreetMap server.

Desktop/Laptop

Click the "OSM" button on the left sidebar to open the OpenStreetMap editor, at your current location.

Mobile

Click the "pencil" icon open your phone's OpenStreetMap editor. (If you don't have one installed, the button won't do anything.)
